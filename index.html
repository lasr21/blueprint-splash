Blueprint
Build your skills. Change the world.
February 21 & 22 @ MIT
Welcome to Blueprint – a two-day learnathon and hackathon held on MIT's campus. Meet MIT’s smartest students and experienced industry mentors as you join fellow high school students in learning how to write impactful software – no coding experience required.

Learnathon
Day 1 - Feb. 21st, 2015

About 100 students will be randomly selected to join us in MIT’s Stata Center on February 21 to learn about topics ranging from developing iPhone apps to making websites. If you have never coded before, don’t worry! MIT students will teach and mentor you along the way in our three tracks: iOS, Android, and Web Development.

At the start of the day, there will be sessions to help you install the right development tools and environments to start coding. The classes will be taught in an interactive lecture format and many mentors will be walking around to help guide you.

Hackathon
Day 2 - Feb. 22nd, 2015

On February 22, about 300 students will gather at MIT’s Media Lab to collaborate on a project of their choice with a team of up to 4 people. But what is a hackathon? A hackathon is an event where people collaborate to create cool apps and software projects!

Teams will be guided by student and industry mentors who are experienced in the field. At the end of the day, you will be able to see what your fellow students created and present your project to a panel of judges. There will many prizes, but most importantly we hope Blueprint is a rewarding hackathon experience for you!
