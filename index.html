February 21 & 22 @ MIT
Welcome to Blueprint – a two-day learnathon and hackathon held on MIT's campus. Meet MIT’s smartest students and experienced industry mentors as you join fellow high school students in learning how to write impactful software – no coding experience required.

Learn
Day 1 - Feb. 21st, 2015
Learnathon @ MIT Stata Center

On February 21, 100 students will join us in MIT's Stata Center for a whole day of learning how to write cool software – no experience necessary.

But what is a learnathon?
Blueprint is MIT's first learnathon – a day of interactive classes and fun workshops designed to teach something new outside of a normal curriculum.

If you have never coded before, don’t worry! MIT students will teach and mentor you along the way in our three tracks: iOS, Android, and Web Development. At the start of the day, there will be sessions to help you install the right development tools and environments to start coding. The classes will be taught in an interactive lecture format and many mentors will be walking around to help guide you.

Hack
Day 2 - Feb. 22nd, 2015
Hackathon @ MIT Media Lab

On February 22, about 300 students will gather at MIT’s Media Lab to create a project of their choice with a team of up to 4 people.

But what is a hackathon?
A hackathon is an event where people collaborate to create cool apps and software projects!

Teams will be guided by student and industry mentors who are experienced in the field. At the end of the day, you will be able to see what your fellow students created and present your project to a panel of judges. There will many prizes, but most importantly we hope Blueprint is a rewarding hackathon experience for you!

FAQ
Is this an overnight event?
No. The learnathon will be on February 21 from around 9 am to 5 pm, and the hackathon will be on February 22 from around 8 am to 8 pm. These times are approximations, and a final schedule will be released closer to the event date.
Where is Blueprint being held?
Blueprint will be held on MIT’s campus. The Learnathon will be held in MIT’s Stata Center and the Hackathon will be held at MIT’s Media Lab.
Who is allowed to come to the Learnathon?
The Learnathon is recommended for students that have little to no programming experience. There will be several classes taught by MIT students on an assortment of topics in our three tracks: iOS, Android, and web development.
Do I register for the Learnathon and Hackathon separately?
There will be an option on your registration application to indicate your interest in coming to the learnathon portion in addition to the hackathon.
What if I don’t know how to code?
That’s perfectly fine! Definitely consider signing up for the learnathon where you will build your programming skills for the Hackathon!
How are participants chosen?
There will be a lottery process where we will randomly choose students in waves until capacity is filled.
How does judging work?
There will be an expo where you may go look at other students’ projects, but you will have a scheduled time to present your project to a panel of judges.
Are there any prizes?
Prizes are yet to be determined, but there will be a lot of them!
Are there any recommended hotels I could stay at if I am from out of town?
A couple of hotels that are near MIT include Kendall Hotel and the Marriott.
What do I need to bring?
Just your laptop, charger, your signed waiver, and a photo ID of yourself with the same name you used in registration!
Does Blueprint cost money?
No. If you get selected, you will just need to show up with the things mentioned in the previous question!
What if I don’t have a laptop?
We will have laptop rentals for those that do not have a laptop. Indicate so on your application, and we will have it for you at check-in.
Is there travel reimbursement?
We are not providing travel reimbursements.
Are my parents allowed to come with me?
Blueprint is for high school students only, but your parents can tune in to the livestream!
What if I don’t have a team?
Don’t feel pressured to have a team prepared when you come. Teams are not required but are highly recommended as they are a great way to meet new people or bond with friends! We’ll have a team formation event at the beginning of Blueprint.
What food options will there be?
We will be providing snacks and all meals for the duration of the event. We will also have food options for those with allergies or dietary restrictions.
I have a question that’s not listed here. Who should I ask?
Send us an email at blueprint@hackmit.org.
