ABOUT

The goal of Blueprint is to teach high school students with basic computer science backgrounds how to develop and code a functional project (from start to finish). We need your help to make this happen! We are looking for undergrads and grads to fill the following roles:

teachers

Learnathon Only (Feb. 27)

  • Lead a hands-on workshop on mobile or web development
  • Workshop should be around 2-3 hours
  • We'll work with you on developing a lesson plan!
  • Or, you can teach a short "tech talk" on a specialized topic in computer science! (Example topics could be computer vision, machine learning, or anything else you're passionate about.)

mentors

Learnathon/Hackathon (Feb. 27/28)

  • On the learnathon day, mentors will act as TAs, moving from group to group and helping them with any questions or roadblocks they have
  • On the hackathon day, mentors will help students with their own hacks
  • Mentorship will be facilitated by HELPq

FAQ

Who can mentor/teach?

We welcome any undergraduate or graduate students interested! We can't provide travel reimbursement, but non-MIT college students are still invited to sign up to mentor and/or teach.

Can I be a workshop teacher and mentor / tech talk teacher and mentor / workshop and tech talk teacher?

Yes! Just indicate so in the form.

Can I help out on Saturday only? Sunday only? Both days?

Yes! Just indicate so in the form.

What kind of background will the high school students have?

Students will have basic, foundational computer science backgrounds. Think AP Computer Science or its equivalent.
